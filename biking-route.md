## Biking Tips & Considerations

### ✅ Pros
- **Fast commute** — 12–15 minutes vs. 8+ minutes by car (when factoring in parking/search time)
- **Low emissions** — zero direct CO₂ emissions
- **Health benefits** — ~50–80 calories burned (bonus exercise!)
- **No parking hassles** — bike racks are abundant near Broadway businesses
- **Cost-effective** — saves money on fuel, parking, and vehicle wear
- **Boulder bike culture** — Boulder is one of the top bike-friendly cities in the US

### ⚠️ Considerations
- **Altitude** — 5,430 ft means thinner air; acclimate if new to high-altitude cycling
- **Return uphill** — the commute south is downhill; the return trip requires pedaling uphill
- **Weather** — Boulder's sun + low humidity means quick drying, but summer monsoons can appear suddenly
- **Safety** — use lights and reflective gear for early morning/late evening commutes
- **Bike lanes** — check current bike lane infrastructure on Pearl Street and Canyon Boulevard

### 🌤️ Best Seasons
- **Spring (Apr–Jun):** Perfect — comfortable temperatures, long daylight hours
- **Summer (Jul–Sep):** Great but early starts recommended to avoid afternoon thunderstorms
- **Fall (Oct–Nov):** Excellent — crisp air, beautiful colors
- **Winter (Dec–Mar):** Possible with fat tires and proper gear; watch for snow-packed bike lanes

### 🔧 Recommended Gear
- **Helmet** — legally required for riders under 18; recommended for all
- **Lights** — essential for Boulder's early sunset times in winter
- **Layerable clothing** — temperature can vary 20°F+ between home and office
- **Water bottle** — high altitude dehydration is real
- **Lock** — secure bike parking is available at most Broadway offices

### 🚦 Boulder Bike Infrastructure
- **Pearl Street Mall** — adjacent bike routes; dismount in the pedestrian zone
- **Canyon Boulevard** — designated bike lanes on sections
- **20th/19th Streets** — check for shared-lane markings (sharrows)
- **BCycle** — Boulder's bike-sharing program for visitors or occasional use
